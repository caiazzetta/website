Sworn translation, also called “certified translation”, aims at legal equivalence between two documents written in different languages. Many institutions such as courts, tribunals, registry offices, universities, etc, require a sworn translation to accept your paperwork. This type of translation guarantees the legal validity of your documents.

The price of a translation is based on the number of words, and the speciality of the source text, for your convenience I've listed some guide prices. Last minute translations may incur extra charges.

The requirements for sworn translations vary from country to country. In Germany:

  • the source document can be shared digitally.
  • sworn translators are not limited by state.
  • sworn translators must have valid credentials, i.e. The Landgericht of Stuttgart authorized me as a sworn translator.
  • translations do not expire, nevertheless source documents may and this is upheld.
  • some institutions have specific rules or guidelines they prefer, please check with the institution you're supplying documents to.
